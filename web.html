Welcome to Makueni County

Makueni County is a county in the former Eastern Province of Kenya. Its capital and largest town is Wote. The county has a population of 987,653.

Makueni County covers an area of 8,034.7 sq km with a projected population of more than 0.9million people. It geographically borders Kajiado County to the West, Taita Taveta County to the South, Kitui County to the East and Machakos County to the North. The county lies in the arid and semi-arid zones of the Eastern region of the country. Major physical features in the county include the Volcanic Chyulu hills which lie along the South West border of the County in Kibwezi West constituency, Mbooni hills in Mbooni sub county and Kilungu hills in Kaiti subcounty. The County is divided into Six sub-counties namely:Makueni,Mbooni, Kaiti,Kibwezi East, Kibwezi West and Kilome.

"Prof. Kivutha Kibwana – Makueni County Governor 2017 – 2022”

Prof. Kivutha Kibwana, Makueni County Governor 2017, Biography, Education Background, Contacts, Life History and Contacts

Biography

Kivutha Kibwana was born on 13 June 1954 in Mwanyani, Nzaui ward, Makueni County, Kenya

Education Background

Went to Nduundune Primary School, He later attended Machakos School (1969-1973) for his O’ and A’ Levels. He obtained a Bachelor degree in Law (Upper Second Class Honours) from the University of Nairobi in 1976 and a Masters of Law. He went to University of London and George Washington University and graduated from Harvard University in June 1984.

Contacts

Email:governor@makueni.go.ke / governor.makueni@gmail.com, Twitter: @governorkibwana,Facebook: Governor Press Service- Makueni County, Website: www.makueni.go.ke

Life History

He was the spokesperson of National Convention Executive Council (NCEC) from 1997 to 2002. In the University of Nairobi, he also served as the Associate Professor (1977-2002), Dean of the Faculty of Law, a senior lecturer and chairman of the Department of private law. In 2002, he became a Member of Parliament for Makueni Constituency, and Minister of both Land and Settlements and Environment & Natural Resources. In January 2008, he was appointed as Presidential Advisor for Constitutional, Parliamentary & Youth Affairs in the President Office and served until September 2012.
